## What to Do After a Car Accident in Pennsylvania

A car accident is stressful no matter how minor. In those first minutes, your adrenaline is pumping and clear thinking isn't easy. This guide walks you through exactly what to do — from the moment of impact through getting your car repaired — with specific advice for drivers in Schuylkill County.

### Step 1: Check for Injuries

Before anything else, check yourself and your passengers for injuries. If anyone is hurt, call 911 immediately. Don't move injured people unless there's an immediate danger (fire, traffic).

Even if you feel fine, be aware that adrenaline can mask injuries. Whiplash, concussions, and soft tissue injuries often don't show symptoms until hours or days later.

### Step 2: Move to Safety (If Possible)

If your car is drivable and you're blocking traffic, move to the shoulder or a nearby parking lot. Turn on your hazard lights. If the car can't be moved, stay inside with your seatbelt on if traffic is a risk, or move well away from the roadway.

On busy routes like Route 61 or Route 209, staying in the road is dangerous. Get off the road if you can.

### Step 3: Call the Police

In Pennsylvania, you're required to report any accident involving injury, death, or vehicle damage that prevents safe driving. Even for minor fender benders, a police report creates an official record that protects you during insurance claims.

For Schuylkill County accidents:
- **Emergency:** 911
- **PA State Police (non-emergency):** Call the local barracks

The responding officer will file a crash report (Form AA-600). You can request a copy from the Pennsylvania Department of Transportation.

### Step 4: Exchange Information

Get the following from the other driver(s):
- Full name and contact information
- Driver's license number
- Insurance company and policy number
- License plate number
- Make, model, and color of their vehicle

Give them the same information. Be polite but don't apologize or admit fault — even saying "I'm sorry" can be used against you later.

### Step 5: Document Everything

Use your phone to:
- **Take photos** of all vehicles from multiple angles
- **Photograph the scene** — road conditions, traffic signs, skid marks
- **Get photos of any injuries**
- **Note the time, date, and weather conditions**
- **Write down what happened** while it's fresh in your memory

This documentation is critical for insurance claims and can save you thousands of dollars.

### Step 6: Contact Your Insurance Company

Report the accident to your insurance company as soon as possible — ideally the same day. Pennsylvania is a "choice no-fault" state, meaning your coverage depends on the policy you chose:

- **Limited tort:** You can recover for medical bills but generally can't sue for pain and suffering unless injuries are serious.
- **Full tort:** You can sue for all damages including pain and suffering.

Your insurance company will assign a claims adjuster. Cooperate with them, but remember — they work for the insurance company, not for you.

### Step 7: Call a Tow Truck

If your car isn't drivable, you'll need a tow. Schuylkill County has several reliable towing companies that offer 24-hour service:

- Call your insurance company first — many policies include roadside assistance.
- If you need a tow immediately, [check our towing directory](/towing/) for local companies.
- Be aware that tow companies charge by the mile. Ask about the total cost before authorizing the tow.
- You have the right to choose where your car is towed. Don't let anyone pressure you into using a specific shop.

### Step 8: Choose a Body Shop

This is where many people make costly mistakes. Here's what to know:

**You have the right to choose your own body shop.** Your insurance company may recommend shops (called "direct repair" facilities), but they cannot require you to use one. Choose a shop you trust.

**Get multiple estimates.** Pennsylvania doesn't require a specific number, but getting 2-3 estimates helps you understand the scope of damage and ensures fair pricing.

**Ask about the warranty.** Reputable body shops warranty their work. Ask about the terms — most good shops offer at least a lifetime warranty on their repairs.

**Check the shop's reputation.** Look for shops that have been in business for years. In Schuylkill County, shops like Kline Brothers (since 1960) and Tom's Auto & Marine (since 1964) have decades of collision repair experience. Browse our [body shops directory](/body-shops/) for options.

### Step 9: Keep Records of Everything

Create a folder (physical or digital) for:
- Police report
- Insurance claim number and adjuster contact info
- All medical bills and records
- Repair estimates and invoices
- Rental car receipts
- Photos from the scene
- Any correspondence with the other driver's insurance

### Step 10: Know Your Rights

Pennsylvania drivers should know:
- **You have 4 years** to file a personal injury lawsuit from the date of the accident.
- **Property damage claims** must be filed within 2 years.
- **Your insurance company must respond** to your claim within a reasonable time. If they're dragging their feet, you have options.
- **You can hire an attorney** if the accident involves serious injuries or disputes about fault. Most personal injury attorneys offer free consultations.

### After Everything Is Settled

Once your car is repaired:
- **Inspect the work carefully** before accepting the car back.
- **Save your repair records** — they'll be important if you sell the car.
- **Follow up on any lingering injuries** with your doctor.
- **Review your insurance policy** — an accident is a good time to make sure your coverage is adequate.

Car accidents are unfortunately common on Coal Region roads, especially in winter. Being prepared with this information can save you time, money, and stress when it matters most.
